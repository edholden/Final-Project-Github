WHY AM I DOING THIS? BY THOMAS KNIGHTS

In the UK, being ginger and male comes with many stigmas attached.

Many are bullied at school and for some, even later in life.

As far as I can see, no one has focused (or noticed) the huge, polarised gap between the way our society perceives red headed women (often the ultimate woman - think Jessica Rabbit) and red headed males (often emasculated and de-sexaulised in ﬁlm and TV). No Hollywood heroes, few leading men.

No other hair colour has this level of gender inequality in any society, and RED HOT holds a mirror up to the pre-conceived limitations we put on the red headed males.

Essentially, what I’m doing is creating an exciting and revealing exhibition showcasing a positive outlook on the red-haired male, and aiming to re-brand the ginger male stereotype along the way.

ABOUT THOMAS KNIGHTS

Thomas Knights began his career as a music producer and performer and quickly realized he also had a talent behind the camera. Inspired by underground visionaries like Helmut Newton, Richard Avedon, Guy Bourdin and David Lachapelle. He has a dark, cinematic yet pop approach to photography, film and music.

His many music videos for the likes of Marina and the Diamonds, Willy Moon, Karin Park and Maya Jane Coles have amassed millions of views online. His photography has been featured in Marie Claire, InStyle, Dazed and Confused and Vogue Italia.

In 2012, Thomas was chosen by Rankin to feature in an exhibition showcasing emerging fashion photographers, and in collaboration with celebrated fashion photographer Tim Bret-Day and nightlife queen Jodie Harsh, he produced a multi-dimensional exhibition featuring one hundred of London’s nightlife characters. The film was showcased exclusively in Love Magazine. He has also shot a Svarovski campaign with the singer Karin Par and his fashion film Tigress Reign was showcased at the Berlin Film Festival.

In 2013 Thomas has shot a major Malibu campaign in Cyprus, two big budget music videos for Maya Jane Coles (he also featured as a singer on her album Comfort). This year has also seen the explosion of his first solo exhibition Red Hot, which has also amassed 30,000 re-blogs on Tumblr alone and has a growing Facebook and Twitter following. He also announced his latest music venture Pandora Drive. This dark disco record will act as a creative vehicle for Thomas to explore his film and photography.

Latest exposure: Love, Dazed & Confused, Hunger, ID, Wonderland, Nylon, Marie Claire, InStyle and Vogue Italia.

Recent advertising clients: Svarovski, Carte Noire, Lacoste and J&J.

Recent music clients: Marina and the Diamonds, Labrinth, Roisin Murphy, Maya Jane Coles, An21Max Vangeli & Tiesto, Tony Bennett, Kitty Daisy & Lewis, Ruby Goe, Karin Park, Foreign Beggars and Willy Moon.
